RC4 Attack Simulation

The Fluhrer-McGrew-Mantin attack exploits weaknesses in RC4's key scheduling algorithm, particularly when used with specific initialization vectors (IVs) in protocols like WEP.

Many protocols have known headers that help with attacks.

The WEP attack exploits weaknesses in the WEP protocol's implementation of RC4, particularly with how IVs are used.

In real attacks, about 40,000-60,000 IVs are typically needed for a successful attack.

RC4 keystreams have statistical biases that can be exploited after analyzing large amounts of ciphertext.

For demonstration only. Real attacks would use billions of samples.
